Section 01 / The frame

Advisor AI training is a three-layer problem before it becomes a curriculum.

About 60% of what a Citi Wealth advisor does every day is database and workflow. Client records, compliance documentation, portfolio reviews, meeting notes. The work is to make that layer faster, not smarter. CitiScribe and Client 360 belong here.

About 30% is rule-based logic. Appropriateness review, fiduciary checkpoints, Regulation BI and state-level fiduciary rules, compensation-aware advice framing. AskWealth CIO sits on this layer when it is scoped properly.

Around 10% is a genuine AI problem. Judgment on cross-border tax structuring, conflicting portfolio signals, or a multi-generational wealth transition. This is the layer where the advisor leans on an AI tool differently, and where the training has to go deepest.

Sorting those three into the right layers, before the role-specific training lands, is the consulting work. The curriculum that follows teaches an advisor exactly where to lean on CitiScribe or AskWealth, and exactly where to put the tool down.

Section 03 / Published research

Ethics Engine is the published companion.

For fiduciary advice, explainability and appropriateness under Regulation BI and SEC rules is the table-stakes framework.
